My Story

I found my competitive spark in FBLA & DECA. As a freshman I became a National Champion, then returned sophomore year to place 4th nationally. Those stages taught me to think fast, build systems, and ship under pressure.

When my mom was diagnosed with breast cancer, that diagnosis lit a fire—pushing me back to nationals. Following that I dove into tech to make real impact. I studied how ML could support clinicians and families.

Since then, I’ve built multiple CNNs and a breast-cancer prediction model. Some early models flopped (spectacularly) — but they taught me why architecture and tuning matter. Now I’m building with purpose, not just accuracy.
